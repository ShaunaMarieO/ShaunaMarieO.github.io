Knitting and Crocheting

The arts of somehow making items with yarn and pointy sticks or a hook.

About the Arts

Knitting

The one that uses a (variety of) needles. Though different from sewing needles, they're still pointy. Don't poke yourself.

A type of craftwork which uses the aforementioned needles by weaving loops of a sort to create a fabric, which may be formed in the shape of a garment or otherwise. Knitting was a method estimated to have been developed during the 11th century CE - the oldest known knitted objects were from then, found in Egypt, and they were complex enough that it's guessed the method had been around even far longer.

Crocheting

The one that uses a (variety of) hooks. These are not precisely pointy, but they still may have sharp edges. Again, don't poke yourself.

Another type of craftwork which (typically) uses a single hook, which weaves loops in a manner to create fabrics, albeit in an entirely different manner from knitting.

Note: A type of crochet known as Tunisian crochet may use more than one of these hooks at a time.

Crochet is newer than knitting, and its first known appearances were in 19th century Europe. The first mention of the specific term was used in a Dutch Magazine in 1823.
